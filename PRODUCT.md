# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owner or GM of a small-to-mid-sized manufacturing shop (10–200 employees). Wears multiple hats — operations, sales, finance. No dedicated IT department. Budget-conscious and skeptical of software promises. Currently running on clipboards, whiteboards, and Excel. When they visit this site they're tired of not knowing what's happening on their floor until the shift is over and the damage is done.

They don't read landing pages top to bottom. They scan for proof that this isn't another platform, another IT project, another salesperson promising ROI they'll never see.

Primary job to be done: understand what's happening on their production floor in real time, without hiring a consultant or buying a $200K MES.

Primary conversion action: **request a demo** — see it working on a real line before committing.

## Product Purpose

ShopFloor Visibility is a fixed-price, tablet-based production tracking service for small manufacturers who need real data without a platform commitment. No sensors, no IT project, no MES. A live dashboard for one machine, line, or process — deployed in 2–3 weeks.

Success looks like: a skeptical plant owner books a discovery call because they believed the page, not because they were sold to.

## Brand Personality

Expert. Confident. Precise.

The page should feel like it was written by someone who has spent time on a shop floor — not a startup founder who Googled manufacturing pain points. No jargon for jargon's sake. No hedging. Say the thing directly.

Emotional goal: the visitor should finish reading and think "this person knows my problem and has already solved it."

## Anti-references

- **Enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, SAP, Plex):** Corporate, feature-bloated, impersonal. The exact platforms we're positioned against. Never feel like a scaled-down version of them.
- **Industrial software (legacy MES, SCADA UIs):** Gray, boxy, no design intent. We're the alternative to that world, not a part of it.
- **Generic Webflow / Framer template:** Stock photos of people at whiteboards, interchangeable SaaS layout, "AI-powered" buzzwords, hero-metric stat grids, gradient text, glowing cards. Looks like it could be any product in any category.
- **VC-funded startup landing page:** Animations for animation's sake, social proof theater, "trusted by 10,000+ teams" with no names attached.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promise.** Every claim earns its place with a specific number, a real quote, or a concrete example. No aspirational copy without a fact behind it.
2. **Respect the reader's time.** The buyer is busy. Short sentences. No filler sections. Every scroll should move them toward a decision.
3. **Expertise without arrogance.** We know manufacturing. We don't explain it to death. We show we understand the problem and move on.
4. **Fixed and fast as a feature.** The constraints (fixed price, 2–3 weeks, one line at a time) are the product's biggest differentiators. Lead with them, don't apologize for them.
5. **Design that earns trust.** The way this page looks and feels is a proxy for how the product will be built. Precise, clean, and intentional — not flashy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigable. All interactive elements have visible focus states. Color is never the only signal (icons + text labels alongside color-coded status). Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 against background. Reduced motion respected — no animations that can't be turned off.
