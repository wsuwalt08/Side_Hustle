Features

Everything you need to see what's happening

Built around the four things that matter: what ran, what stopped, what failed, and why.

Tablet Production Entry
Operators log good parts, reject parts, shift, product, and operator on a tablet during the shift — not from memory afterward. Fast buttons, simple layout, no training friction.
Downtime Reason Buttons
When the line stops, operators tap the reason in real time — not recalled at shift end. See a Pareto of top stop causes by shift, operator, and machine automatically.
Live Dashboard
A real-time shift dashboard on any screen — tablet, laptop, or wall TV. Production vs. target, top downtime reasons, reject rate, and efficiency by shift.
Export & Reports
Every entry is exportable to Excel or CSV. Daily or weekly summary reports included. A written improvement summary at pilot close — what the data shows, where to focus first.

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need a PLC or existing automation?

No. The system uses tablet-based manual entry by operators. No PLC integration, no sensor hardware, no IT project. Works on any machine regardless of age or brand.

How is this different from Amper or Pulsar?

Amper and Pulsar use IoT sensors and require $30k+ to get started. This is a fixed-scope pilot on one process using tablets. Usable data in weeks, not quarters, at a fraction of the cost.

What if I want to expand to more lines?

The pilot proves value first on one process. Expansion to additional lines is quoted separately — you'll have real data to justify it internally.

Do operators need training?

Yes — and it's included. On-site operator training is part of every pilot. Most operators are comfortable with the tablet form within one shift.

What does optional monthly support cover?

Dashboard updates, new reason codes, form changes, export assistance, and a monthly data review. Not required — you own the system after the pilot.

What area do you serve?

DFW metro and up to 75 miles. Setup and training are on-site. Some remote configuration is possible but the kickoff and training are in person.
